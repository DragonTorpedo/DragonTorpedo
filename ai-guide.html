Prompt Library

Battle-Tested Prompts

Copy, paste, adapt. Each prompt is a starting template — adjust the brackets to your context.

Reasoning

"Think step by step before answering. First, restate the problem in your own words. Then list the key facts and constraints. Then propose two approaches, compare them, and only then give your final answer."

Writing

"Rewrite this in the voice of [author]. Keep the substance, change the style. Pay attention to sentence rhythm, word choice, and the way they handle transitions. Then explain what you changed and why."

Code Review

"Review this code as a senior staff engineer would. Focus on: correctness, edge cases, performance, readability. For each issue, give the line, the problem, and a concrete fix. End with the top 3 priorities."

Analysis

"Analyze this document. Extract: (1) the core claim, (2) the evidence given, (3) what's missing, (4) the strongest counterargument, (5) one experiment that would test the claim. Be terse."

Learning

"Teach me [topic] as if I were a smart beginner. Use analogies. After each section, ask me a question to check my understanding before moving on. Don't proceed until I answer."

Decision

"I'm deciding between [A] and [B]. Help me think. List the criteria that should matter, weight them, score each option, then play devil's advocate for the winner. End with a recommendation and your confidence level."
